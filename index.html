Jackson Park

Our tour begins at the edge of Jackson Park, the future home of the Obama Presidential Center. The park was originally designed by Frederick Law Olmsted for the 1893 World's Columbian Exposition.

Stony Island Arts Bank

We head south to 68th and Stony Island. This restored 1920s bank building, saved from demolition by artist Theaster Gates, is now a library, archive, and gallery space.

63rd Street Corridor

Finally, we move to the 63rd Street corridor. This area is seeing a surge of residential and commercial growth, anchored by its proximity to the University of Chicago and the Metra line.
