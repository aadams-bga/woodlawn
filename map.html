Woodlawn is a community of about 26,000 people on Chicago’s South Side, just south of the University of Chicago.

The community area, a well-defined and historic region, is primarily residential and deeply rooted in South Side history.

The area is near world-class cultural hubs like the Griffin Museum of Science and Industry and the DuSable Black History Museum.

The Obama Presidential Center

Joining them soon will be the Obama Presidential Center — a massive complex housing Barack Obama’s presidential library and foundation offices alongside community and athletic facilities.

But the center’s development has exacerbated neighborhood changes that are forcing longtime residents out as the area becomes more white and housing costs rise.

“Four new, nearly identical newly-built and boxy homes, each valued at $1 million, stand out against the brick multifamily units on the same block” — South Ellis Avenue.
