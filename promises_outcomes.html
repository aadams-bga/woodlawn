Vacant Lot Set Aside
The Promise

52 city-owned vacant lots reserved for new housing with units affordable to residents making $17,500 per year.

The Outcome

The city sold two lots. One housing development is completed.

PEAR Program
The Promise

$1.5M in grants to buy or refinance multifamily properties in exchange for 30-year affordability agreements.

The Outcome

No applications were received. The $1.5M in funding was "reallocated," according to DOH.

Renew Woodlawn
The Promise

$500,000 granted to nonprofit developer POAH to acquire and redevelop residential units in Woodlawn.

The Outcome

To date, POAH has not used the money.

Woodlawn Loan Fund
The Promise

$7 million loan fund for local developers to bring vacant lots and apartment buildings back online.

The Outcome

40 units were financed. No single family home projects got off the ground.

Home Improvement Grants
The Promise

$1 million granted to long-term homeowners in Woodlawn who need help fixing up their properties.

The Outcome

36 homeowners received grants up to $20,000.

Hiring Requirements
The Promise

City required developers on city-owned land to submit plans for local hiring, outreach, and training.

The Outcome

One developer submitted a plan for 19 jobs and hired three people.

Right of First Refusal
The Promise

Give Woodlawn renters the first chance to match or beat outside offers on their buildings before a sale goes through.

The Outcome

To date, no tenants have exercised their right of first refusal.
